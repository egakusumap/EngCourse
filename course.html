Continuous Tense

Learn how to use the continuous tense in English.

What is Continuous Tense?

The continuous tense, also known as the progressive tense, is used to describe actions that are ongoing at the time of speaking.

Present Continuous Tense

The present continuous tense is formed using the present tense of the verb "to be" (am/is/are) + the base verb + -ing.

  • I am eating dinner.
  • She is watching TV.
  • They are playing soccer.

Past Continuous Tense

The past continuous tense is formed using the past tense of the verb "to be" (was/were) + the base verb + -ing.

  • I was reading a book.
  • He was cooking dinner.
  • We were playing video games.

Future Continuous Tense

The future continuous tense is formed using will be + the base verb + -ing.

  • I will be traveling to Paris next week.
  • She will be studying for her exams tomorrow.
  • They will be meeting us at the restaurant.
